# Research Question Framing

## Provisional Lane
**Search Intelligence – Content Refresh Prioritization**

## Research Question
How can we identify webpages that are at risk of declining search performance so they can be prioritized for timely content updates and optimization?

## Unit of Analysis
The unit of analysis is an individual webpage. Each page will be evaluated independently using its search performance, engagement metrics, and content-related features.

## Expected Output
The project will produce a ranked list of webpages based on their likelihood of requiring content refresh. Each page will receive a priority score that can help teams focus on the most impactful updates first.

## Business Decision
The objective is to help SEO and content teams decide which webpages should be reviewed and updated before their search performance declines significantly.

## Recommended Action
Pages identified as high priority should be reviewed for content quality, freshness, keyword relevance, metadata, and overall user experience. These recommendations are intended to support content planning and optimization efforts.

## Cost of Incorrect Recommendations
Recommending healthy pages for updates may lead to unnecessary effort and inefficient use of resources. On the other hand, failing to identify genuinely declining pages could result in reduced search visibility, lower organic traffic, and missed business opportunities.

## Why Machine Learning?
Search performance is influenced by multiple factors, including impressions, click-through rate (CTR), average ranking position, content freshness, and historical update patterns. Since these factors interact in complex ways, machine learning can help uncover meaningful patterns that are difficult to capture using fixed rules alone. The model is designed to support data-driven decision-making while keeping human review as part of the final process.

## Notes
This research question serves as an initial problem statement for the capstone project. It may be refined during the internship as additional insights are gained from data exploration, mentor feedback, and subsequent analysis.
